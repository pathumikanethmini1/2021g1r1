PREPOSITION

Referance/Preposition

What are prepositions?

A preposition is a word or group of words used before a noun, pronoun, or phrase to indicate direction, time, place, location, spatial relationships, or to introduce an object. Simply preposition is a word or phrase that connects a noun or pronoun to a verb or adjective in a sentence.prepositions can tell us where or when something is. However, prepositions are much more versatile than that. The word "preposition" means "positioned before." A preposition sits before a word (either a noun or a pronoun) to point out that word's relationship to another nearby word. prepositions are a closed class of words which implies no new preposition gets added to the language. We use a fixed set of prepositions.most of the prepositions have many various contextual and natural uses. So, it's easy to be confused about preposition.prepositions don't have any other form. they can't be plural, possessive, inflection, or anything else.sometimes a preposition works as nouns, adjectives, and adverbs.



Types of prepositions


1)Simple preposition

Simple prepositions are the short words used to determine a relation between nouns, pronouns or even to join parts of a clause or sentence.

Examples : At, by, as, but, for, from, in, into, than, of, off, on, out, over, till, to, up, upon, with, under, down


2)Double preposition

The double preposition is a proposition that is made by combining two simple prepositions.

Examples : Into, upon, onto, throughout, inside, without, from within, out of, from behind, outside of, from above, up to, next to, according to, because of, as above, up against, at about, at around, over to, over from, off on, off to


3)Compound preposition

Compound prepositions are those prepositions that are made by prefixing the preposition to a noun, an adjective or an adverb.

Examples : amongst, amidst, above, around, along, across, about, below, beneath, beside, between, beyond, outside, within, without.


4)Participle preposition

Participle preposition is a verb ending with '-ing', '-en' or '-ed', which also acts as a preposition.

Examples : failing, considering, regarding, during, accepting, expecting, following, excluding, regarding, respected, provided, pending, according, barring, between, given, taken, including, owing
